Cricket is believed to have been invented in South East England in the 16th century.
The first official international cricket match was played between the USA and Canada in 1844.
A cricket pitch is exactly 22 yards (20.12 meters) long between the wickets.
Sachin Tendulkar holds the record for the most runs in international cricket, with over 34,000 runs.
The longest recorded cricket match took place in 1939 between England and South Africa; it lasted for 12 days and eventually ended in a draw.
Muttiah Muralitharan from Sri Lanka holds the record for the most wickets in Test cricket, with 800 wickets.
The Cricket World Cup is held every four years; the first tournament was organized in 1975 in England.
A standard cricket ball weighs between 5.5 and 5.75 ounces (155.9 to 163 grams).
The highest team total in a Test match is 952/6 declared, scored by Sri Lanka against India in 1997.
Sir Donald Bradman has a career Test batting average of 99.94, which is widely considered the greatest achievement by any sportsman in any major sport.
